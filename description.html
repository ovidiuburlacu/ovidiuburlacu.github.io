Non-Zero-Sum Games

Non-zero-sum games are not strictly competitive, they also have cooperative elements. Players engaged in a non-zero sum conflict have some complementary interests and some interests that are completely opposed.

Prisoner's Dilemma Theory

Two criminals are captured by the police. The prisoners are put in separate cells with no way to communicate with one another and each is offered to confess.
If neither confess, both get convicted to 1 year.
If both confess, both are sentenced to 5 years.
If one confesses and the other does not, the one who confess is granted immunity, the other is senteced to 20 years.

Payoff

On one hand, is tempting to confess. But the best outcome for both prisoners is when neither confesses.

Iterative

In the iterative version, the players may remember if the other prisoner confessed or not, and decide accordingly. Would you forgive and collaborate with someone who betrayed you, for a better outcome for both?
