Game Theory

Almost every game has some form of prisoner's dilemma. When players colaborate with one another, the payoffs for all are greater than when they fight each other.

Geopolitics

The Cold War is an example of prisoner's dilemma. Countries opted to colaborate (i.e. not use nuclear weapons).

Theory of Evolution

Scientists found that prisoner's dilemma can explain why altruism and social cooperation developed for some species.

Business

Competing businesses setting the price of their products is an example of prisoner's dilemma: if only one company lowers the price, it will gain more market share and gain more profits. But if the competition lowers the price as well, both companies are worse off.
